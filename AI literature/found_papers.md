# Topic 8: AI Literature Search Results

As requested, here is the list of highly relevant, verified academic papers concerning asymmetric latency, visual cue mismatch, and entanglement in online choirs. The papers have **not** been downloaded. You can review the abstracts via the links provided.

## Found Papers

1. **How Late is Too Late? Effects of Network Latency on Audio-Visual Musical Collaboration** (IEEE VR, 2022)
   - **URL:** https://rishivanukuru.com/assets/documents/papers/ieeevr2022.pdf
   - **Relevance:** Directly addresses the cue mismatch when fast audio and slow video make musical collaboration difficult.

2. **Studying the Effects of Network Latency on Audio-Visual Perception During an AR Musical Task** (2022)
   - **URL:** https://www.researchgate.net/publication/366626178_Studying_the_Effects_of_Network_Latency_on_Audio-Visual_Perception_During_an_AR_Musical_Task
   - **Relevance:** Discusses how visual cues are relied upon following pauses but become a liability under network delay.

3. **VRChoir: Exploring Remote Choir Rehearsals via Virtual Reality** (IEEE VR, 2023)
   - **URL:** https://danielpiressm.github.io/assets/papers/IEEEVR23_VRChoir.pdf
   - **Relevance:** Examines synchronous choir rehearsals overcoming visual asynchronicity and audio latency issues.

4. **'Entanglement' - A new dynamic metric to measure team flow** (Gloor et al., 2021)
   - **URL:** https://arxiv.org/pdf/2112.00538
   - **Relevance:** The foundational Peter Gloor paper explicitly defining the mathematical metric for entanglement and team flow.

5. **Architecture Design of a Networked Music Performance Platform for a Choir** (Springer, 2022)
   - **URL:** https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-3-031-15743-1_40
   - **Relevance:** Specific case study on conducting remote rehearsals for a medium-sized academic choir.

6. **Exploiting Latency In The Design Of A Networked Music Performance Environment** (NIME)
   - **URL:** https://nime.org/proceedings/2025/nime2025_69.pdf
   - **Relevance:** Interesting counter-approach on designing systems that accommodate rather than eliminate latency.
