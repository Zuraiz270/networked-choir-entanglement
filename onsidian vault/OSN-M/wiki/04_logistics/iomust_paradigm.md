# Internet of Musical Things (IoMusT)

The Internet of Musical Things (IoMusT) is an emerging paradigm where computing devices are embedded in physical musical objects (instruments, interfaces, wearables) to enable real-time collaborative production and reception of musical content.

## Characteristics
- **Stable Flow:** Unlike general IoT (erratic sensors), IoMusT requires a continuous, high-bandwidth stream of periodic packets.
- **Deterministic Processing:** Requires low-jitter, real-time operating systems (e.g., Elk Audio OS) to minimize "Digital Processing Delay."
- **Social Connectivity:** Extends beyond simple audio transfer to include "Performative Conditions" (eye contact, gesture sync, and collective "flow").

## Role in SNA-OSN-M
Our project is a software-defined implementation of the IoMusT vision. We treat the browser (WebAudio/MediaPipe) and the dedicated hardware (SoundJack/JackTrip) as "Musical Things" whose entanglement we measure via the $E(t)$ index.
