## Core Process: Scrum-Based Iterations

The seminar uses **Scrum phases** per iteration:
1. Iteration Planning Part 1 (WHAT — with customer/mentor) + Part 2 (HOW)
2. Daily Scrums (suggestion: bi-weekly)
3. Retrospective (keep / drop / try format)
4. Demonstration in virtual meetings

---

## Key Tips for Distributed Teams

**1. Get to know each other first** — share who you are, hobbies, background. Take time for personal-level connection before diving into work.

**2. Discuss project AND personal goals** — what each member can contribute + what they want to learn. Align on scope with all stakeholders; do not assume shared understanding.

**3. Agree on ways of working**:
- Decide regular meeting slots upfront (e.g., 2/week)
- Write down decisions after each meeting
- Use a common repository (Dropbox, Google Docs, etc.)
- Communicate often, answer fast; prefer synchronous (Skype, Slack, chat)
- Promise only what you can deliver
- Raise problems and ask for help immediately

---

## Communication Recording Protocol

- **CC all email communication** to the project's shared email address.
- For face-to-face / virtual meetings: send ONE email to the whole team immediately after, labelled `f2f` in subject.
- Email body must include: Participants · Start & End Time · Outcome (main decisions).
- This data is needed for the team's own communication analysis (after Iteration 2).

---

## Iteration Presentation Template

Each status presentation should cover:
1. Goals of the project
2. Progress during the last iteration: what was done, main results
3. Goals and plans for next iteration
4. Retrospective output (keep / drop / try)
5. Problems / questions
