## Cohort-Wide Pattern (Key Finding)

From the full Student Profiles sheet (~25+ students, Uni Bamberg + Uni Köln + HSLU):

- **Symbiont**: Dominant archetype cohort-wide is **Capybara** (harmonizer). Most students got Capybara regardless of institution. Exceptions: a few Ant profiles (e.g., Timo Ercole, Yannick Herrmann, Simona Kals, Marie-Luise Schweizer, Hassan Ahmed, Zuraiz).
- **SC Chat / Network position**: Almost universally **Tree Hugger** across all students.
- **Perceptiface**: Most common dominant emotion = **Neutral** or **Sad**. Few outliers (e.g., Laurin Strobel: Angry; Julius Schneider: Angry; Hassan Ahmed: Happy — notable positive outlier).
- **Beecome**: High Agreeableness and Conscientiousness are the modal results.

**Implication for Q&A**: Our team's Sad/Neutral face divergence from Ant-type communication is a cohort-wide phenomenon, not an individual anomaly. Multiple students across chapters noted surprise at their Perceptiface result. Context effects (Zoom fatigue, exam stress, novelty of being recorded) likely explain the negative-valence face signals.
