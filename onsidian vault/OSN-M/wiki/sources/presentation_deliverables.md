## Slide-by-Slide Script Digest

### Slide 15 — Tool Integration Framework · `[0:00–1:30]`

Three-step presenting scaffold (resolves the open question in [[chapter_14_presentation]] slide 15):

1. Report each tool output without interpretation.
2. State what Chapter 14 predicts those numbers *should* look like (stable signals should line up across tools).
3. Highlight where tools disagree — that disagreement is the discussion starter.

### Slide 16 — Four-Tool Live Evidence · `[1:30–3:30]`

Placeholders from the pptx now filled with Tree Huggers actual data (see [[Team_Profile]] for the authoritative 4-tool table).

**SC Chat** — all three = Tree Hugger, Ant-scores 73% / 64% / 63% (Zuraiz / Hammad / Hassan).
**Symbiont** — Zuraiz **38% Ant / 38% Capybara** (honest mixed reading, not forced to one label · resolves Open Question in [[Team_Profile]]) · Hassan 40% Ant · Hammad 49% **Capybara**.
**Response latency** — Hammad **1.99 hours** vs Zuraiz 16 min vs Hassan 21 min. Frames Hammad's Capybara archetype as "thinks before responding," with the honest counter-question: *thoughtful leadership, or low engagement?*
**Perceptiface** — Sad 22% (Zuraiz) / Neutral 23% (Hammad) / Happy 20% (Hassan). Three different emotions on a tool that claims to reveal personality.
**Beecome** — Zuraiz + Hassan high Agreeableness; all three high Conscientiousness. Converges with SC Chat.

**Alchemical pipeline narrated in-place**: Nigredo = personal WhatsApp group chat since last year (study, exam prep, project coordination — not an artificial sample). Albedo = feature extraction (who, when, how fast). Citrinitas = classification (Tree Hugger / 73% Ant). Rubedo = the live human judgment about Hammad's 2-hour latency.

**Core claim**: face captures the moment; chat + game capture the person. The divergence is structural, not anomalous.

### Slide 17 — Discussion Q1 · `[3:30–5:30]`

*Is the alchemical-stage ↔ ML-pipeline mapping structural, or a metaphor?*

**Team fallback answer**: The same four steps appear in plant classification, horse emotion detection, and human team dynamics — three domains, one pipeline. Structure seems real. But "alchemy" may be the helpful metaphor-name rather than a literal claim.

### Slide 18 — Discussion Q2 · `[5:30–7:30]`

*If one archetype dominates 80% of the cohort, did the model discover something — or does it lack resolution?*

**Team fallback answer** introduces two cohort quotes not previously in the wiki:
- **Sarah Löhnert**: *"Everyone is always a Capybara."*
- **Michael Sova**: same.

Our team's long-running (>1 year) WhatsApp chat still produced the same dominant archetype as everyone else — strong evidence the tool may be measuring **platform communication style** rather than individual personality. Sharpens the Capybara Puzzle in [[socialcompass_tools]].

### Slide 19 — Discussion Q3 · `[7:30–9:00]`

*Where is this Nigredo→Rubedo pipeline deployed in the real world, and where does it fail?*

**Team fallback answer**: **HireVue** — facial emotion AI screening job candidates. Same pipeline structure (record face → extract features → classify → decide) but *no Rubedo*. No human checked whether the classification made contextual sense; conflated cultural expression with competence. Eventually pulled. Illustrates Chapter 14's warning: without the human interpretation stage, the pipeline produces confident-looking output that is not meaningful.

### Slide 20 — Close · `[9:00–9:30]`

Conscientious team. Three tools agreed independently. Face data diverged — but at cohort scale, that's context, not personality. Pattern becomes judgment. Judgment is ours, not the model's.

## Q&A Prep (3 Canned Responses)

| Anticipated question | Response strategy |
|:---|:---|
| *"Perceptiface=Sad but SC Chat=stable Ant — contradiction?"* | Perceptiface captures seconds; SC Chat captures months. Cohort-wide Sad/Neutral dominance suggests measurement-context effect, not personality. Three behavioral tools agree. |
| *"How do the four tools measure the same thing?"* | They don't — they measure the same person from four angles (language, network, face, behavior). Cross-tool convergence (3/4) is where honest signal emerges. Perceptiface's disagreement tells us which layer is momentary. |
| *"If everyone is Capybara, does the tool even work?"* | Honest concession: when one archetype covers 80%, can't distinguish "most students are harmonizers" from "WhatsApp makes everyone sound like a harmonizer." Acknowledging this is exactly what Rubedo asks for. |
