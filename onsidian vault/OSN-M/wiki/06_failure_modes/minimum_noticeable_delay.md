# Minimum Noticeable Delay (MND)

Minimum Noticeable Delay (MND) is the highest amount of lag between two stimulated senses (e.g., the sound of a drum and the sight of the hit) that can be experienced before they are perceived as separate, non-simultaneous events.

> **Same-study note (2026-04-24):** [[p_09_how_late]] (IEEE VRW 2022 workshop abstract, 2 pages) and [[s_04_ar_latency_perception]] (ISMAR 2022 full paper, Hopkins et al.) are **the same study at two venues**. S-04 is the full report with F-statistics; P-09 is the abstract. The frequently quoted "1200 ms tolerance" is a **cyclic-rhythm-confound artifact** at the 1200 ms test condition (delayed beat aligns with the next cycle of the repeating rhythm), not a real perceptual ceiling on free-rhythm tasks.

## Benchmarks for NMP

Research (Hopkins et al., 2022, S-04 full paper) identifies the MND for AR-based musical collaboration as falling between **160 ms and 320 ms**.

| Delay (ms) | Perception |
| :--- | :--- |
| < 160ms | Subliminal; integration is automatic. |
| 160ms - 320ms | Noticeable; brain starts to detect a "smearing" of the event. |
| > 320ms | Discrete; the audio and visual are processed as separate events. |

## Impact on "Flow"
MND represents the boundary of "Immersive Entanglement." Beyond the MND, the performer is no longer in a unified "space" with their partner, but is consciously managing two different temporal streams.
