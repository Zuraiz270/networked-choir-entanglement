# Vocal Entrainment

Vocal Entrainment refers to the adaptation of paralinguistic features of speech—such as pitch, rhythm, tone, and speech rate—between interacting individuals.

## Musical Context
In choral settings, vocal entrainment is the primary auditory mechanism for $A(t)$ (Audio Coupling). Singers adjust their **vibrato rate**, **vowel color**, and **onset timing** to match their neighbors.

## Failure Mode
Network latency disrupts vocal entrainment. If a singer hears their partner 300ms late, they may subconsciously slow down their own tempo to "wait" for the partner, leading to a "tempo drag" spiral where the ensemble progressively slows down until the rhythmic structure collapses.
