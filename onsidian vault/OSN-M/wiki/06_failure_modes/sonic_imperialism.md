# Sonic Imperialism

Sonic Imperialism refers to the dominance of Western musical standards—specifically 12-Tone Equal Temperament (12-TET)—embedded in digital communication technologies.

## In NMP Platforms
Conventional tools (Zoom, Skype) are optimized for Western speech and 12-TET music. Their compression algorithms:
- **Flatten Microtones:** Erase the pitch nuances of Indian Ragras or Middle Eastern Maqams.
- **Quantize Rhythms:** Distort complex African poly-rhythms by prioritizing the 4/4 "grid."
- **Normalize Timbre:** Strip the harmonic richness of non-Western instruments (e.g., panpipes, sitar).

## Research Implication
When analyzing "Entanglement" in a global context, our $A(t)$ (Audio Coupling) metric must be tuning-agnostic. We cannot assume a 12-TET grid for $A(t)$ without risking culturally biased results.
