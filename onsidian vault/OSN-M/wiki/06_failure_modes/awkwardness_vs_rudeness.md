# Awkwardness vs. Rudeness

A critical finding in NMP and videoconference research (Chang et al., 2025) is the distinction between **Gaps (Awkwardness)** and **Interruptions (Rudeness)** in terms of their impact on group cohesion.

## The Hierarchy of Failure
1. **Gaps (The "Awkward" Zone):** Silent periods where no one knows whose turn it is. These are rated as the *most* damaging to group enjoyment. In NMP, this often happens when a singer stops due to a latency glitch, and others wait for them to recover.
2. **Interruptions (The "Rude" Zone):** Simultaneous overlapping speech/singing. While detrimental to *fluidity*, they are rated as *more tolerable* than gaps because they indicate active engagement, even if uncoordinated.

## Project Strategy
Our "Sonic Imperialism" mitigation strategy should prioritize **Minimizing Gaps** over **Avoiding Overlaps**. In a high-latency network, it is better for the system to encourage "continuous flow" (even if slightly out of sync) rather than "stop-and-start" behavior that induces high-level social awkwardness.
