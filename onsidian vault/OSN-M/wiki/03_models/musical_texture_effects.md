# Musical Texture Effects on Synchronization

The "texture" of a musical piece heavily dictates the social and leadership structure of the performing ensemble. This structure directly impacts the measurable physical synchronization (e.g., PLV) between group members.

## Homophonic Textures (Clear Leadership)
- **Definition:** Rhythmically differentiated melody and harmonic accompaniment.
- **Leadership Structure:** Highly centralized. The melody instrument assumes an unambiguous leadership role, and accompanying instruments serve as followers.
- **Physical Manifestation:** Lower overall visual synchronization. Followers may only need to attend to the leader rather than mutually adapting to the entire group.

## Polyphonic Textures (Ambiguous Leadership)
- **Definition:** Multiple independent melodic parts proceeding simultaneously (e.g., countermelodies).
- **Leadership Structure:** Egalitarian or roaming. Leadership is distributed throughout the ensemble.
- **Physical Manifestation:** Heightened mutual adaptation, anticipation, and joint attention. This results in significantly stronger interpersonal coupling and higher measured physical synchronization across the entire network graph.

## Structural Boundaries
The effects of these textures are amplified at phrase boundaries (beginnings and endings). At phrase entries, timing uncertainty is high, forcing ensembles playing polyphonic textures to rely heavily on visual cues (head nods, body sway) to negotiate the entrance, causing massive spikes in bodily synchronization.
