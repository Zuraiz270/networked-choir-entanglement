# Hyperscanning

Hyperscanning is the simultaneous recording of neural activity (EEG, fMRI, or fNIRS) from two or more individuals as they engage in a shared task or interaction.

## In Musical Synchronization
Hyperscanning has been used to study "inter-brain connectivity" during joint musical performances (e.g., duo piano playing). It reveals that when performers are highly "entangled" or in flow, their neural patterns in motor and sensory areas show significant phase-alignment.

## Theoretical Limit of SNA-OSN-M
While our current project focuses on behavioral (Audio/Visual) synchronization, Hyperscanning represents the ultimate ground truth of "Mental Entanglement." We use behavioral proxies (like head sway PLV) because they correlate strongly with the neural alignment found in hyperscanning literature.
