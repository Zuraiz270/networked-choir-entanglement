# Respiratory Synchrony

Respiratory Synchrony is the temporal alignment of breathing cycles among members of a group, particularly critical in choral singing.

## Mechanisms
- **The Collective Inhale:** Before a phrase starts, singers must synchronize their inhalation to achieve a unified vocal attack.
- **Phase Locking:** During long polyphonic sections, singers may "phase lock" their breathing to the musical meter or to a neighbor's physical cues.

## Inclusion in E(t)
We define the **Respiratory Index $B(t)$** as the cross-correlation of chest expansion curves extracted via structured light (P-16) or markerless MoCap (P-18).
- $B(t) \approx 1$: High biological entanglement; preparation for unified performance.
- $B(t) < 0.5$: Biological fragmentation; likely to result in staggered entries and poor rhythmic cohesion.
