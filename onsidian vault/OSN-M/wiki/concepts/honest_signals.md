# Honest Signals

Pentland's term ([[Alex_Pentland]], MIT 2008) for the unconscious body-language, timing, and vocal-rhythm cues humans emit during collaboration. Four dimensions in his original framework: **influence**, **mimicry**, **activity**, and **consistency**.

## Why "Honest"

The signals are called honest because they are mostly **involuntary** — below the level of conscious control — and therefore less susceptible to strategic manipulation than verbal content.

## Relevance to Project 8

The V(t) component of [[entanglement_index]] operationalizes Pentland's honest-signals intuition for choir video:

- **Activity**: trunk-sway amplitude and mouth-aperture velocity.
- **Mimicry**: pairwise cross-correlation of trunk-sway and mouth-aperture across singers.
- **Consistency**: variance of inter-singer xcorr over time.
- **Influence**: maps onto N(t) via Granger-causal onset networks.

Hypothesis H3 (see [[Project_Overview]]) tests whether adding Pentland-style visual features increases ΔR² ≥ 0.10 over audio-alignment-only models.

## Caveat

Pentland's original validation was on face-to-face business meetings and negotiations, not music. Our adaptation to choir performance is a domain transfer, not a validated application. See [[limitations_register]].
