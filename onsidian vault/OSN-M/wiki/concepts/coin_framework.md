# COIN Framework (Collaborative Innovation Networks)

Peter Gloor's model (Oxford, 2005) for **distributed groups self-organizing toward a shared creative goal**. A COIN is characterized by high internal trust, voluntary membership, transparent communication, and a shared ethical code.

## Structure

Three concentric rings:

1. **Core COIN** — small group of committed innovators.
2. **CLN** (Collaborative Learning Network) — expanded ring sharing learning and iterating on the core's output.
3. **CIN** (Collaborative Interest Network) — outer ring of interested observers and latent contributors.

## Relevance to Project 8

Two distinct applications:

1. **Object of study**: online choirs are a clean case of a CIN / CLN self-organizing around a shared performance. Gloor's lens treats the choir as the COIN; our job is to measure its coordination.
2. **Meta-level**: the SNA-OSN-M seminar requires the team itself to be treated as a COIN — see the Virtual Mirror deliverable (2026-05-21).

## Measurement

Gloor's [[s_02_entanglement]] paper proposes the Entanglement formula EA(xT, yT) = [CD(xT)·CD(yT)] / d(A(xT), A(yT)) as a COIN-coherence metric. **Validated on email with 7-day windows** — adaptation to continuous music streams is a novel domain transfer (see [[entanglement_index]]).
